Id like to fit a classification tree to see the relation between type of home and other variables. I will put some extra information in our tree so that we can see the classification rate, and somehow estimate its performance.

set.seed(850)
HousetypeTree = rpart(type_of_home~., data = HousetypeData, control = rpart.control(cp = 0.001), method = "class")
prp(HousetypeTree, extra=102, split.fun=split.fun, under=T)

The tree is not so fine (because its kind of complicated).

Lets see how well our tree performs. For that well use confusion matrix.

The tree has become (slightly) less complex and easier to interpret. Classification rates increased and thats for good. Lets try to interpret the tree. If the householder status of a particular person is either own or rent, then he lives in the house. If its not: Then if annual income is more or equal to $20,000, number of persons is more than 4, type of home is house. If the number of persons living in the household is less than 4 then the household is apartment. If the householder status is rent and the amount of people is less than 3 then its apartment. If the householder status is rent and the income is greater or equal to $20,000, number of persons is more than 6 its a house. If number of people is less than 6 its an apartment.
